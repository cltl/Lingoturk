About this study

In this study, you will be presented with a full sentence and a short statement about vaccinations. You will be asked the following:

  1. Does the author of the sentence ('John') agree or disagree with the statement?
  2. Do the quoted sources in the sentence (if any) agree or disagree with the statement?
By participating in this study, you help researchers to train and evaluate AI systems that can automatically detect conflicting information across texts.


The labels

AGREEMENT Based on the sentence, John must believe that the statement is true.
DISAGREEMENT Based on the sentence, John must believe that the statement is false.
PARTIAL AGREEMENT Based on the sentence, John must believe that the statement is only partially true.
I CANNOT TELL (a) The statement is about something else than the sentence (e.g. safety vs. effectiveness).
(b) It cannot be determined what John believes.
(c) I don't understand the statement.
Some examples with explanations can be found during the task.

General tips

  • You will often be able to infer from the sentence whether John is pro- or anti-vaccination. Avoid making a guess based on this information. Instead, ask yourself the question: Based on what is written in the sentence, is it possible for John to believe the statement?
  • The statements are partially automatically generated; try to ignore grammatical or lexical inconsistencies. If you really cannot understand the statement, choose I cannot tell.
  • For quoted people/sources, base your answer on what John thinks they believe, not on what you think they actually believe.
